Pain Creature

by nar.interactive

Pain Creature is an interactive dance performance that explores the communication of bodily discomforts and the dynamics of witnessing others' pain. The dancer's chronic pain is represented by an interactive costume called the soma extension - a Pain Creature. Pain Creature is an unwanted presence, a parasite living on the dancer's skin that responds to touch with abstract sonic utterances. The performance unfolds as a dialogue between the dancer and the creature, accompanied by a texture of evolving electronic sounds.

about the artists

nar.interactive is an art performance duo composed of Dila Demir and Vincenzo Madaghiele. They explore themes of embodied memories and cultural identities combining electronics, bodily materials, algorithms, and textiles into performances and installations.

Their work focuses on the development of interactive textile objects mediating personal experiences and cultural narratives through auditory-tactile interactions. The duo's influences include electronic glitch, noise and metal music, Anatolian rug weaving, somaesthetics, phenomenology of movement, feminist sci-fi novels and southern Italian folk rhythms.
