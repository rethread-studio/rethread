by Yuvia Maini

This interactive installation discusses the individual pursuit of "presence" in Western mindfulness culture. Stemming from ancient collective traditions, the work explores what happens when the path to inner peace is commodified. Through real-time EEG data, the participant’s brain waves are translated into live imagery that mirror their "level of relaxation." As visitors attempt to meditate and make their way to “Nirvana”, the piece highlights the paradox of measuring tranquility in an attention-driven economy.

The installation is open for interaction between 17:00 and 19:00 on 04/10, 05/10 and 12/10. During other times visitors are invited to relax in a prerecorded meditation session of the author's mind.

about the artists

Yuvia Maini is a multidisciplinary animator and artist working within immersive storytelling. Her practice blends non-traditional narratives and participatory technologies to examine contemporary cultures defined by perpetual screen time. She has created public interactive artworks around Sweden and worked on commissions for Swedish Parliament, Håkan Hellström, Kristianstad Municipality, Sala Municipality. She is a part of Rumtiden Idea Lab and Misschiefs.
